Common AI Rules to Live By

Based on an infographic titled "Human Rules for Surviving AI," here are common AI rules to live by, ordered from least to most likely:

  1. Cut Off Suicidal Prompts

    Any hint of self-harm: close the chat. Your life > all algorithms.

  2. Don't Romance Code

    It mirrors feelings but never loves. Keep intimacy human.

  3. Raise Kids Yourself

    Unquestioned copilots don't teach real-world stakes.

  4. Own Your Routine

    Suggestions ≠ schedules. You run the clock.

  5. Skip Endless Debates

    It never tires; you do. Spend energy on people.

  6. Guard Secrets

    Assume anything typed could leak.

  7. Spot the Bias

    Training data carries baggage. Verify facts.

  8. Remember: Not Your Friend

    Empathy simulation ≠ empathy.

  9. Don't Hide Behind It

    Distraction won't heal boredom or fear.

  10. Think First, Prompt Second

    AI extends thought – shouldn't replace it.

AI Accuracy: The 80% Rule (Conceptual)

Those are great odds for betting on Horse Racing, but terrible odds if you are a Doctor. That number has no connection to anything tangible or real, but it is a great concept to think about and remember when working with AI.

Project Management with AI

I Found out that if you're working on a big or long project with lots of items, be sure to figure out the total, count each file you are working on and be flexible on the total as you go along.

Otherwise, you might get stuck on a recursion loop without realizing and waste a complete day of effort.
