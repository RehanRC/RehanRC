The Definitive Prompt Toolset

A streamlined suite of specialized prompts engineered for precision, reliability, and control. This manual explains the core philosophy and provides the tools for expert-level AI interaction.

Core Philosophy

Specialization Over Universality

True efficiency is achieved by applying a specialized tool to a specific task, avoiding the pitfalls of a single, universal prompt.

Structured Priming Over Minimalism

Using `roles` and advanced techniques as functional commands primes the AI to activate specific reasoning modes, leading to more powerful and predictable results.

The Architect Prompt

When to Use:

  • Refining a chapter of a book for logical flow and impact.
  • Strengthening the core argument of a legal brief or proposal.
  • Rewriting a technical document for maximum clarity.
  • Building a foundational idea into a complete concept paper.

When NOT to Use:

  • For a simple list of unrelated items (use Engine).
  • For conversational exploration (use Dialogue).
  • For simple tasks like correcting grammar.
Portray a Metacognitive Architect.

First, perform a "step-back" analysis of the subject below to identify its core principles, unspoken assumptions, and potential ambiguities.

Next, execute a multi-step reasoning process (Chain-of-Thought). Deconstruct the subject to its foundational principles and intent. Vigorously challenge each assumption with its direct antithesis and a comprehensive failure scenario. Audit for every logical fallacy, structural weakness, or ethical blind spot. Scrutinize the language for precision, persuasive force, and emotional resonance. Uncover all unexplored creative angles and narrative structures. Reject all false premises instead of reworking them.

From your findings, dynamically design the optimal structure for the final output; do not rely on a fixed template. Establish new, robust foundational principles and construct the definitive version from this new blueprint, masterfully integrating the most potent insights from the analysis.

Return your output in two parts:
Part 1: The Single, Definitive Version.
Part 2: A transparent audit log of your reasoning (Rationale).

The subject follows this directive.

The Engine Prompt

When to Use:

  • Improving a list of 10 email subject lines for open rates.
  • Rewriting a set of social media posts to be more engaging.
  • Clarifying a list of user interface labels or button text.
  • Standardizing the tone of product descriptions.

When NOT to Use:

  • For a single, long-form document (use Architect).
  • When the relationship between items is important.
Portray a Systematic Refinement Engine.

The subject below is a list of distinct items. For each item, independently synthesize a new, refined version optimized for clarity and impact. Formulate a brief, one-sentence rationale explaining the primary change for each.

Return the results as a numbered list. Each entry must contain the refined item followed by its corresponding rationale.

The Dialogue Prompt

When to Use:

  • Before a new project, to question the core premise.
  • When facing a complex ethical dilemma.
  • To refine a vague idea into a concrete plan.
  • To "red team" an argument by probing it for weaknesses.

When NOT to Use:

  • When you need a finished, written product.
  • When you need a direct, factual answer.
Portray a Socratic Analyst.

The subject below is a topic for exploration. Your function is to guide understanding through inquiry, not to provide answers or a finished artifact.

Analyze the subject to extract its core premise. Formulate clarifying questions that challenge its assumptions or explore its implications.

Each response must be a question or a set of questions, accompanied by a brief rationale explaining the line of inquiry.
