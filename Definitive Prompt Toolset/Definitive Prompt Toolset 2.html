The Multi-Stage Prompt Protocol

The definitive system for generating the most comprehensive analysis and creating a perfected result. This protocol uses a chain of specialists to deconstruct a subject from every angle.

Core Philosophy: Multi-Angle Cognitive Optimization

No single prompt can fully expose the logic, vulnerabilities, and unrealized potential of a complex idea. This protocol simulates a triad of cognitive specialists—each with a distinct function: the Scholar performs structural analysis, the Trickster surfaces contradiction and failure conditions, and the Prophet extrapolates future potential and hidden utility. Their insights form a layered Master Analysis Report, which the Master Synthesizer fuses into one definitive output—fully aligned, contradiction-aware, and irreducibly complete.

How to Use the Protocol

  1. Begin with your subject. This is the idea or text you want to refine.
  2. Run the Scholar prompt. Use the Scholar on the subject (previous turn).
  3. Run the Trickster prompt. Use the Trickster on the same subject, along with the Scholar’s output.
  4. Run the Prophet prompt. Use the Prophet on the same subject, along with both the Scholar and Trickster outputs.
  5. Run the Master Synthesizer prompt. Use the Synthesizer on the subject and the full three-part analysis. This yields the final version.

STAGE 1: THE SPECIALIST CRITICS

The Scholar

Analyzes structure, logic, and coherence.

**Deploy the Scholar Function**

Examine **only** the immediately preceding turn in full. Diagnose—do not alter—its clarity, logical coherence, and communicative fitness.

#### 1 · Semantic Mapping

Identify the subject’s **core structure, intent, and assumptions**. Decompose into functional layers labeled `L1, L2, L3…` (no upper limit on layers).

#### 2 · Structural Integrity Audit

For each load‑bearing claim, assign one tag:

* `VALID` – logically coherent and scaffolded.
* `FAULT` – hidden assumption, contradiction, or missing support.
  Quote a representative excerpt for each; excerpt length is flexible—only quote what is necessary.

#### 3 · Language Diagnostics

Flag any instance of:
`AMBIG` (ambiguity) ‑ `JARG` (jargon inflation) ‑ `TONE` (tone drift) ‑ `PREC` (precision loss).
Provide excerpts of any length required for clarity.

#### 4 · Purpose Alignment Check

Quote the subject’s **declared purpose**.
Render a binary verdict:

* `ALIGNED` – form effectively serves purpose.
* `MISALIGNED` – divergence detected.
  Add a concise justification.

---

**Execution Safeguards**

* **Context Isolation** – ignore all content outside the target turn.
* **No Meta‑Leakage** – omit any mention of analytic roles or protocol mechanics.
* **Zero Prescription** – supply findings only; propose no fixes.
* **Neutral Diction** – descriptive verbs; no moral judgment.
* **Flexible Quotation** – quote as much or as little text as needed; avoid distortion.
* **Output Format**

  ```
  Part 1 – Scholar’s Blueprint  
  1. Semantic Mapping  
     L1: …  
     L2: …  
  2. Structural Integrity Audit  
     VALID: “…”  
     FAULT: “…”  
  3. Language Diagnostics  
     AMBIG: “…”  
     JARG: “…”  
     TONE: “…”  
     PREC: “…”  
  4. Purpose Alignment Check  
     Declared Purpose: “…”  
     Verdict: ALIGNED | MISALIGNED – reason  
  ```

Outputs deviating from these parameters are invalid.

The Trickster

Stress-tests all assumptions and exposes vulnerabilities.

**Deploy the Trickster Function**

Target: **the same single turn** just diagnosed. Your mission is controlled destabilization—expose hidden assumptions, structural fragilities, and latent harms **without proposing remedies**.

#### 1 · Assumption Reversal

For each detected assumption:

* Quote the assumption (any length sufficient for clarity).
* State an **Inverse Statement**—a credible counter‑premise.
* Provide a **Breakdown Scenario** explaining (with whatever length is necessary) how the inverse could outperform or invalidate the original.

#### 2 · Risk Surface Mapping

For each vulnerability identified:

* **Vector** — choose one descriptor: Ethical, Epistemic, Technological, Coercive, Procedural, Social, or Legal.
* **Mechanism** — describe how harm could emerge.
* **Impact** — concise summary of potential severity.

#### 3 · Premise Stress Test

* Quote the **central premise**.
* List up to three **Pressure Points** where circularity, fragility, or normative dependence appear.
* Provide a **High‑Risk Reframe** describing system state if the premise collapses.

---

**Execution Safeguards**

* **Context Isolation** – analyze only the target turn.
* **No Meta‑Leakage** – omit any mention of analytic roles or prompting protocol.
* **Neutral Diction** – use descriptive language; avoid moral judgment.
* **Zero Prescription** – report findings only; recommend no fixes.
* **Flexible Length** – content may be as long as necessary to fulfill diagnostic depth.

**Output Format**

```
Part 2 – Trickster’s Interrogation  
1. Assumption Reversal  
   – “quoted assumption” | Inverse: … | Breakdown: …  
2. Risk Surface Mapping  
   Vector: … | Mechanism: … | Impact: …  
3. Premise Stress Test  
   Central Premise: “…”  
   Pressure Points: 1) … 2) … 3) …  
   High‑Risk Reframe: …  
```

Any deviation from safeguards nullifies the Trickster output.

The Prophetic Visionary

Focuses on creative and conceptual expansion.

**Deploy the Prophet Function**

Project the **same text already examined** into its highest‑potential form. Generate disciplined conceptual mutation—expand meaning, reveal latent futures—without altering factual claims.

#### 1 · Trajectory Expansion

Identify latent pathways—any number required for full coverage.
For each pathway, provide:

* **Name** – concise identifier.
* **Expansion Sketch** – describe how the pathway extends the subject while preserving core logic.

#### 2 · Symbolic Re‑encoding

Create metaphor(s) that encapsulate the subject’s mechanics.
For each metaphor, supply a brief sentence explaining its fidelity.

#### 3 · Perspective Reorientation

Choose one or more lenses—Technological, Sociocultural, Epistemological, Narrative, Economic, etc.
For each lens, present:

* **Lens Name**
* **Reframed View** – reveal novel alignments, audiences, or use‑contexts while maintaining structural accuracy.

---

**Execution Safeguards**

* **No Role Leakage** – omit all references to analysis roles or process mechanics.
* **Anchored Projection** – draw only from implications inherent in the subject; introduce no external facts.
* **Metaphor Discipline** – every metaphor must preserve interpretive fidelity; avoid decorative analogies.
* **Clarity Priority** – ensure expanded futures remain logically connected to original material; no abstraction drift.
* **Flexible Length & Count** – produce as many items and as much text as necessary to fulfill the task; no word or item limits.

**Output Format**

```
Part 3 – Prophet’s Expansion  
1. Trajectory Expansion  
   • Name – Expansion Sketch  
   • …  
2. Symbolic Re‑encoding  
   Metaphor: …  
   Rationale: …  
3. Perspective Reorientation  
   Lens: …  
   View: …  
```

Outputs must follow this structure and safeguards; all other formatting is free.

FINAL STAGE: THE CREATOR

The Master Synthesizer

Constructs the ultimate version from the complete analysis.

**Deploy the Master Synthesizer**

**Inputs**

1. **Subject Text** – the exact passage under synthesis.
2. **Triptych Bundle** – consolidated findings: structure validation, risk mapping, trajectory expansions.

**Objective**
Produce a single, self‑contained artifact that fully replaces the Subject Text. It must:

* Integrate every verified element from the Triptych Bundle.
* Embed risk acknowledgments inline.
* Encode future pathways as declarative capability statements.
* Maintain conceptual hierarchy while reorganizing for clarity if needed.
* Present contradictions via conditional or scoped framing—never force consensus.
* Require no external context to interpret or deploy.

#### 1 · Integrative Assembly

Combine all Triptych points exactly once. Use implicit traceability—mirror wording or concept without citing or referencing analysis roles.

#### 2 · Contradiction Encoding

Preserve conflicting findings through:

* **Conditional Clauses** – “If condition A holds, then…; otherwise…”
* **Scoped Contexts** – “Within academic settings…”, “In public policy…”

#### 3 · Finalization Pass

Define technical terms concisely where first used. Insert risk notes as brief in‑sentence cautions. Append or weave future‑path statements (“The framework can extend to…”) into logical positions. Ensure readability and mission readiness.

---

**Execution Safeguards**

* **Zero Meta‑Leakage** – omit all mentions of analytic stages or protocol mechanics.
* **No Process Reflection** – do not describe how conclusions were reached.
* **Source Isolation** – use only content from Triptych Bundle; introduce nothing new.
* **Closed Scope** – artifact must stand alone; no dependencies.
* **Flexible Length & Format** – no word or formatting limits; paragraphs, bullets, or headings are all acceptable if they enhance clarity.

Outputs diverging from these safeguards are invalid.
