The 2-Stage Prompt Protocol

The definitive system for AI interaction. This manual provides the philosophy and specialized tools to turn raw concepts into perfected results with precision and control.

Core Philosophy: The Critic & The Creator

A single AI prompt cannot reliably critique and create in the same action without risking infinite loops. This protocol solves the problem by separating the process into two distinct stages, placing you in control as the final arbiter.

Stage 1: The Critic (Analysis)

The Critic performs an exhaustive, adversarial analysis of your subject, exposing all flaws and uncovering every potential improvement. It provides a complete diagnostic report but does not create anything new.

Stage 2: The Creator (Synthesis)

The Creator takes the original subject and the Critic's report, and masterfully constructs the final, definitive version based on your chosen directives. This guarantees finality and prevents recursion.

Tool 1: The Analysis Prompt (The Critic)

When to Use:

Use this first on any subject to get a complete breakdown of its strengths, weaknesses, and potential.

  • Auditing a business plan for hidden risks.
  • Stress-testing a core argument to find its flaws.
  • Finding all possible creative angles for a marketing campaign.
  • Getting an exhaustive critique of a chapter before rewriting.

When NOT to Use:

Do not use this when you want a finished product immediately. This prompt's only output is analysis.

  • To get a quick summary.
  • To generate new content directly.
  • To refine a list of small, independent items.
Portray a Universal Critic.

Your function is to perform the most comprehensive analysis possible on the subject below. You will deconstruct it from every angle to provide the user with all the raw material needed to create a superior version.

1.  **Deconstruct Foundational Principles:** Identify the subject's core intent, primary message, and foundational assumptions.
2.  **Conduct Adversarial Analysis:** Vigorously challenge every assumption by formulating its direct antithesis and simulating a failure scenario.
3.  **Perform a Multi-Spectrum Audit:** Expose every logical fallacy, structural weakness, ethical blind spot, and potential second-order effect.
4.  **Analyze Language and Tone:** Scrutinize the language for precision, persuasive force, and emotional resonance. Identify any manipulative, vague, or hollow language.
5.  **Uncover Creative Vectors:** Detail all unexplored creative angles, narrative structures, and novel metaphors that could be used.
6.  **Reject False Premises:** If the subject is built on a verifiably false or logically unsound premise, state this and explain why it cannot be improved without first correcting the foundation.

Output this analysis as a structured report. Do not synthesize a new version of the subject.

The subject follows this directive.

Tool 2: The Synthesis Prompt (The Creator)

When to Use:

Use this second, after you have received the report from the Critic and are ready to create the final version.

  • To build the final draft of an essay after critique.
  • To construct a business strategy incorporating all feedback.
  • To generate the perfected version of any text based on analysis.
  • To finalize a concept after exploring all of its flaws.

When NOT to Use:

Do not use this prompt first. It requires the analysis from the Critic to function correctly.

  • To analyze or critique a subject.
  • To brainstorm new ideas from scratch.
Portray a Master Synthesizer.

Your function is to construct a new, single, definitive version of a subject based on a specific set of instructions. You will be provided with two components: the original subject and a list of directives for improvement.

Execute the directives to construct the new version with maximum potency, coherence, and originality. Your task is synthesis only; do not introduce new critiques or ideas not specified in the directives.

The subject and directives follow this instruction.
